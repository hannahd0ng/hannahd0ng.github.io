Annotated
Bibliography

Collage has redefined traditional artistic practices and has given artists a medium to challenge social, political, and cultural structures, all of which have greatly influenced the evolution of modern art. Collage, which originated with Picasso and Braque, has allowed artists to manipulate reality with newfound tools of dynamic richness, primarily reinforcing collage as an everyday material such as old newspapers, photographs, and found objects from the beginning up to our current digital day. Based on the example of one such female artist, Hannah Höch, who tackled gender norms and social expectations using her photomontages, collage here becomes a powerful medium to engage with social statements. This study aims to understand theoretically and visually how collage is used as a tool of artistic expression, political resistance, and cultural reflection. As a transformative art form, collage has evolved from its early 20th-century Dada origins to become a powerful tool for social commentary, challenging traditional artistic boundaries and reflecting the complexities of modern identity and culture.
