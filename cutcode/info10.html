Larson, Haley A. "Writing that Reads: Collage Poetics and Aesthetic Techniques as Media Literacies." PhD diss., The University of Iowa, 2020.

Larson examines how collage has evolved from its early use in the Dada movement to modern iterations in contemporary art. The subject of this study is collage, an indispensable medium for depicting political and social issues, which features works by artists dealing with mixed media. The book's artists use collage to explore identity, consumerism, and where the merging of culture and tech will lead. This is a significant source; it's invaluable to know how collage can still evolve over a cultural landscape shift, specifically in the digital age. Moreover, it enables us to see the collage in different forms and how various approaches to materials, textures, and visual languages can vary. Collage, within contemporary practice, provides a powerful device for expressing the complexity of modern life and challenging the status quo of the artistic norms, says Larson.
