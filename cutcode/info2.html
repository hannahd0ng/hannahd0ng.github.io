Höch, Hannah, Peter W. Boswell, Maria Martha Makela, Carolyn Lanchner, and Kristin Makholm. "The Photomontages of Hannah Höch." (No Title) (1996).

This book explores Hannah Höch’s photomontages, emphasizing her pivotal role in Dadaism and her influence on feminist art. In their analysis of Höch’s innovative use of collage to challenge gender norms, political structures, and the societal burdens heaped on women in early 20th-century Europe, the authors show her importance. Her fragmented, layered compositions challenge current artistic forms while engaging with social and cultural themes, which the book discusses. Through juxtaposing images of women, political figures, and everyday life, Höch questioned authority and the dominant narratives of her time. The authors also discuss the context of Höch’s artistic development in the Weimar Republic and under the influence of the Dada movement. Höch's works as a tool of feminist resistance, and the appreciation of how they participated in the discussion about identity, gender, and societal expectations in modern art makes this book invaluable.
