Morrill, Rebecca, Yuval Etgar, and Giovanni Aloi. "Vitamin C+: collage in contemporary art." (No Title) (2023).

This is a critical resource for exploring the role of collage in today's artistic landscape. The book traces the development of collage from its origins in Dadaism and Surrealism to its contemporary use in digital and mixed media art. The book is the work of numerous contemporary artists, exploring how collage is still an important and innovative medium in the digital age. Collage allows for deconstructing and reassembling reality, as if modern life is fragmented. Understanding how collage is still an effective vehicle for political or social commentary and how artists employ it in pursuing issues such as identity and culture, technology, and consumerism is vital to this source. Through its examination of work by contemporary practitioners, the book shows how collage can continue its challenge of artistic convention and engagement with changing cultural narrative. This source is essential for anyone researching collage and modern art, as it is also unique.
