Dalton, Laurie. "Critical Collage." Journal of Canadian Art History/Annales d'histoire de l'art Canadien 43, no. 1/2 (2022): 168-183.

In this article, Dalton examines how collage evolved into a key medium for social and political commentary. Drawing from Picasso and Braque's early debut of collage within the context of Cubism, he shows how this increased artistic expression opportunities. Collage's ability to combine disparate elements into a single composition gave it special power to reflect modern life changes brought about by economic development, war, and even social changes. In addition, Onibere talks about how collage for artists became a way to contest traditional ideas about aesthetics and representation. The article reflects on artists' social use of collages to dissect political happenings and social imperatives. This study is invaluable to understanding the significance of how collage as a revolutionary art form has shaped both modern and contemporary artistic practices.
