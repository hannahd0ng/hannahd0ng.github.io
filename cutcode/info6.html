Wahl, Christy Ann. Dada under Occupation: The Works of Hannah Höch, 1933–1949. The University of Wisconsin-Madison, 2021.

This text explains Hannah Höch’s role in the Dada movement and her lasting impact on feminist art. In the photomontage scenes, she combined cut-and-paste elements from mass media to make strong parodies of mass society. Höch depicted tensions concerning gender inequality, political disputes, and the dilemmas relating to identity through her collages. Detailed analysis of Höch’s artistic process demonstrates how her art broke down conventions of art and society. The authors point out that Höch’s collages expressed the fragmentation of the modern world and, through their use of fragmented imagery, provided new points of view on gender and politics. This source is key for anyone interested in the convergence of feminism and collage: it sheds light on Höch’s role in making modern art and feminist theory.
