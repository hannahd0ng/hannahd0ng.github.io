Maeekalle, Mirjam. "Everything is a collage." PhD diss. (2023).

Maeekalle's article focuses on the political and social functions of collage and how collage became a key medium for subverting norms and a reflection of the fracture of modern life. With time, collage allowed artists to no longer think about traditional forms of art but instead to make works very much suggestive of a changing world. Maeekalle's analysis traces the evolution of collage, from its early use in Cubism to its expanded role in Dadaism, Surrealism, and Pop Art. This article critically examines how Höch and others used collage to critique gender roles, political power structures, and the media. This resource is especially useful for its application to the role of collage as a medium for subversive art. For anyone interested in the connection between art and social change, Maeekalle's insights are especially valuable.
