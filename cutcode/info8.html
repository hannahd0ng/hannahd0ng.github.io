Banash, David C. "From advertising to the avant-garde: Rethinking the invention of collage." Postmodern Culture 14, no. 2 (2004).

This article examines how collage emerged as a revolutionary medium in modern art, discussing its role in transforming traditional artistic practices. It emphasizes how modern life was broken down and reassembled by artists, from Picasso to the present day, essentially using collage to dismantle visual reality. The article discusses how collage was employed to critique political and cultural structures, with specific attention to Dadaism and Surrealism. Through everyday materials, artists could protest against the generally accepted societal norms and traditional artistic boundaries. Not only does the article discuss how, after college, collage became a way to explore questions around identity, politics, and consumerism in later Pop Art movements. This source helps us understand how collage works as a medium for artistic expression, social commentary, and critique. It furnishes a crucial background for the historical evolution of collage as a device to spur the artistic movement.
